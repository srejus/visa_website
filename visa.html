US Visa - 9500* INR

The USA tourist visa, also known as the B-1/B-2 visa, grants non-U.S. citizens and permanent residents the opportunity to travel to the United States for various reasons such as tourism, vacation, visiting family or friends, medical treatment, or participating in social events or activities.

The main objective of the USA tourist visa is to provide individuals with the chance to temporarily explore the United States for leisure, tourism, and other non-business purposes. It is important to note that this visa does not authorize individuals to engage in work or study activities within the USA.

Typically, the B-2 visa allows visitors to stay in the United States for a maximum of six months per visit. However, the actual duration granted may vary depending on the discretion of the U.S. immigration officer at the port of entry.

Canada Visa - 9500 INR


The Canada Tourist Visa, also known as a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV), is designed for non-Canadian citizens or permanent residents to travel to Canada for tourism, visiting family or friends, or other short-term reasons. This visa allows individuals to visit Canada temporarily for leisure, tourism, or other short-term purposes.

Multiple entry visitor visas enable holders to travel to Canada for up to six months at a time, with the flexibility to enter the country as many times as desired while the visa remains valid. These visas can have a validity period of up to 10 years, although the specific duration is determined by the visa officer responsible for issuing it.

UK Tourist Visa - 9500 INR


The UK Tourist Visa, formally known as the Standard Visitor Visa, is designed for individuals who want to visit the UK for a short period. You can usually stay in the UK for up to 6 months. You might be able to apply to stay for longer in certain circumstances.

Tourism and Leisure: Visitors under this category come to enjoy the UK's tourist attractions, historical sites, cultural events, and natural beauty. No work or business activities are allowed.

Visiting Family and Friends: This category is for those who wish to spend time with family or friends living in the UK. The visa holder must provide details of their host and evidence of the relationship.

Business-Related Visits: Business visitors can attend meetings, conferences, training sessions, and briefings, or conduct site visits. They cannot engage in paid or unpaid work outside the scope of their business activities.

Medical Treatment: Individuals seeking medical treatment in the UK must provide proof of medical arrangements and sufficient funds to cover the cost of the treatment and stay. Extensions are possible if further treatment is required.

Short-Term Study: Students can take short courses in the UK. They must provide evidence of their course enrollment and proof of funds to support their stay.

Participating in Events: Athletes, artists, entertainers, and other cultural participants may enter the UK to participate in events or performances. They must provide evidence of their participation and support for their stay.

Transit Visa: This is for individuals who are traveling through the UK to another destination. They need to show evidence of their onward travel and the necessary documentation for their destination country.

Switzerland Visa - 6500 INR


The Schengen C-type visa grants access to the 26 European countries that form the Schengen Area, allowing for a wide range of authorized travel purposes, from tourism to business meetings. The specific visa issued, whether single-entry or multiple-entry, is tailored to the applicant's itinerary and travel requirements.

As part of the Schengen Area, Switzerland follows the same visa rules as the other countries in the zone. This implies that people coming from nations not belonging to the European Union (EU) or the European Free Trade Association (EFTA) might have to get a Schengen Visa to come to Switzerland for vacation.

Schengen Visas are usually given for the period of stay they are intended for and cannot be renewed. Should travelers wish to stay in Switzerland or the Schengen Area for more time, they need to leave the Schengen Zone and go through the process of getting a new visa if needed.
